Burrito

A burrito is a delicious food item that breaks down all social barriers and leads to temporary spiritual enlightenment. - Lisi Harrison

Ingredients:

Ingredients Amounts
Sunflower oil 1 tbsp
Onion (finely chopped) 1
Garlic clover (finely chopped) 4
Ground cumin 1 tbsp
Ground coriander 1 tbsp
Cayenne pepper A pinch
Dried oregano 1 tbsp
Beef bince 500g
Golden caster sugar A pinch
Wine (or cider) vinegar 1 tbsp
Chopped canned tomatoes 400g
Black (or kidney) canned beans 400g (plus the can water)
Flour (or corn) tortillas 8
Cooked rice 500g
Sliced avocado (or guacamole) As many as you like

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan – a casserole is ideal. Fry the onions for 8 mins, then add the garlic, spices and oregano and cook for 1 min. Crumble over the mince and sizzle for 5 mins, stirring, until browned. Stir in the sugar and leave for a minute, then splash in the vinegar and pour in the tomatoes
  2. Simmer for 5 mins then tip in the beans and the water from the can. Season, stir and simmer everything for 20 mins until the beef is in a thick gravy. The sauce can be prepared up to 2 days ahead, chilled and reheated with a splash of water or frozen for 6 months
  3. To make the burritos, heat the tortillas following pack instructions. Pile some rice and beef sauce along each tortilla and scatter over your choice of topping. Fold over the ends and roll up to seal. Secure by wrapping with foil if you want. Eat immediately

Tips

  • This also goes well with pitta bread or stuffed mushrooms
  • You can add your own sauces and use a different filling if you like

Citrus Spiced Salmon

The Salmon is the most stately fish that any man may angle to in fresh water - Dame Juliana Berners

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Dried edible roses petals (finely ground) 4 tbsp
Sumac 2 tbsp
Dried lime powder 1 tbsp
Ground cumin 1 tbsp
Cinnamon 1/2 tbsp
Orange (zest only) 1
Lime (zest only) 1
Olive oil 3-6 tbsp
Skinless salmon fillets 4 (150g each)
Sea salt As much as you link
Black pepper (freshly ground) As much as you like
Lemon 1 (sliced into 6)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6
  2. Line a baking tray with baking paper
  3. Combine the ground rose petals, spices, orange zest and lime zest in a small bowl
  4. Add the olive oil and stir well to form a wet marinade for the salmon
  5. Rub each salmon fillet well with the marinade and place on the tray (At this stage you can cover with clingfilm and leave overnight in the refrigerator to marinate, if you wish)
  6. Once all the fillets are well coated in the marinade, season generously with sea salt and black pepper
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through
  8. Serve with lemon wedges

Tips

  • If possible use a fan assisted oven for this recipe, as a higher heat gives better results
  • Don't overcook so the juices are preserved

Source: BBCFood

Spanish Omelette

Be content that those who can make omelettes properly can do nothing else - Hilaire Belloc

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
New Potato 500g
Onion (preferably white) 1
Extra virgin olive oil 150ml
Chopped flat-leaf parsley 3 tbsp
Eggs 6

Steps

  1. Scrape the new potatoes (or you can leave the skin on if you prefer)
  2. Heat 150ml extra-virgin olive oil in a large frying pan
  3. Add the potatoes and onion and stew gently, partially covered, for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally until the potatoes are softened
  4. Strain the potatoes and onion through a colander into a large bowl
  5. Beat 6 eggs separately, then stir into the potatoes with 3 tbsp chopped flat-leaf parsley and plenty of salt and pepper
  6. Heat a little of the strained oil in a smaller pan
  7. Tip everything into the pan and cook on a moderate heat, using a spatula to shape the omelette into a cushion
  8. When almost set, invert on a plate and slide back into the pan and cook a few more minutes
  9. Invert twice more, cooking the omelette briefly each time and pressing the edges to keep the cushion shape
  10. Slide on to a plate and cool for 10 minutes before serving

Tips

  • If your worried about the omelette breaking when sliding into the pan use two plates and place them on top each other to turn the omelette
  • Make sure your hands aren't oily when turning the plates
  • Add rosemary to get a bit more flavour

Ratatouille

Ratatouille doesn't sound delicious. It sounds like 'rat' and 'patootie.' Rat-patootie - Lou Romano (Linguini) in Ratatouille (2007)

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Large Aubergines 2
Small courgettes 4
Red (or yellow) peppers 2
Large ripe tomatoes 4
Olive oil 5 tbsp
Basil A small bunch
Medium Onion (peeled thinly sliced) 1
Garlic cloves (peeled, crushed) 3
Red wine vinegar 1 tbsp
Sugar (any kind) 1 tbsp

Steps

  1. Cut 2 large aubergines in half lengthways
  2. Place the sliced aubergines on a board, cut side down, slice in half lengthways again and then across into 1.5cm chunks
  3. Cut the ends off 4 small courgettes, then across into 1.5cm slices
  4. Peel peppers from stalk to bottom
  5. Hold upright, cut around the stalk, then cut into 3 pieces, remove any membrane, then chop into bite-size chunks
  6. Score a small cross on the base of each of 4 large ripe tomatoes, then put them into a heatproof bowl
  7. Pour boiling water over the bowl, leave for 20 secs, then remove
  8. Quarter the tomatoes, scrape away the seeds with a spoon, then roughly chop the flesh
  9. Set a sauté pan over medium heat and when hot, pour in 2 tbsp olive oil
  10. Brown the aubergines for 5 mins on each side until the pieces are soft then set them aside
  11. Fry the courgettes in another tbsp oil for 5 mins, until golden on both sides then repeat with the peppers
  12. Tear up the leaves from the bunch of basil and set aside
  13. Cook 1 thinly sliced medium onion in the pan for 5 minutes
  14. Add 3 crushed garlic cloves and fry for a further minute
  15. Stir in 1 tbsp red wine vinegar and 1 tsp sugar
  16. Tip in the tomatoes and half of the basil
  17. Return the vegetables to the pan with some salt and pepper and cook for 5 mins
  18. Serve with the remaining basil

Tips

  • Add a deseeded and finely chopped chilli with the garlic for an extra kick
  • For a more intense Mediterranean flavour, add 1 tbsp capers, a handful of pitted black olives and a few chopped anchovies

Crème Brûlée

Finding love is like making creme brulee. It may take a few tries before you get it right - Crystal Woods

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Double cream 600ml
Vanilla pod 1
Large egg yolk 8
Caster sugar 3 tbsp
Demerara sugar 6 tbsp (approx.)

Steps

  1. Put a pie dish of about 20cm / 8 inches diameter in the freezer for at least 20 minutes
  2. Put the cream and vanilla pod into a saucepan and bring to boiling point, but do not let boil
  3. Beat the eggs and caster sugar together in a bowl
  4. While still beating, pour the flavoured cream over it, pod and all
  5. Rinse and dry the pan and pour the custard mix back in
  6. Cook over medium heat (or low, if you're scared) until the custard thickens (should take around 10 minutes)
  7. When the cream's thick enough, take out the vanilla pod, retrieve the pie dish and pour this creme into the severely chilled container
  8. Leave to cool, then put in the fridge till truly cold
  9. Sprinkle with demerara sugar, spoonful by spoonful, and burn with a blowtorch till you have a blistered tortoiseshell covering on top
  10. Put back in the fridge if you want, but remember to take it out a good 20 minutes before serving
  11. Just before serving, put the bowl on the table and, with a large spoon and unchecked greed, crack through the sugary carapace and delve into the satin-velvet, vanilla-speckled cream beneath

Tips

  • Start by filling half of your the sink with cold water. This is just a precaution in case the custard looks as if it's about to split, in which case you should plunge the pan into the water and whisk the custard
  • This is a very difficult dessert to complete so make sure to proceed with caution and do each step steadily

Source: Nigella

Raspberry Coconut Ice Cream Sundae

In my films, all the great things are put together. It's not like one kind of ice cream but rather a very big Sundae - George Lucas

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Raspberries 250g
Icing suagr 1 tbsp
Dark chocolate 100g
Double cream 200ml
Raspberry ripple ice cream 8 scoops
White chocolate and coconut ice cream 8 scoops
Cononut (shaved) 30g

Steps

  1. To make the coulis, place 150g of raspberries in a small pan with the icing sugar and heat for a few minutes until the raspberries collapse and start to release their juices
  2. Tip everything into a bowl and blend briefly with a hand blender until the raspberries are puréed
  3. Pass through a fine sieve to remove the seeds and reserve in the fridge
  4. For the ganache, break up the chocolate and place in a glass bowl
  5. Bring the cream to a simmer in a saucepan, then pour over the chocolate and mix until glossy
  6. To make the sundaes, spoon some coulis into the bottom of two tall glasses, add a scoop of the white chocolate and coconut ice cream and swirl some ganache around the glass
  7. Add a layer of fresh raspberries and a spoon of coulis followed by two scoops of the raspberry ripple ice cream
  8. Swirl over some more ganache and top with fresh raspberries and coconut slices to serve

Tips

  • Swap out the ice cream flavor and fruit for ingredients you prefer if you like
  • Top each sundae with a crispy waffle

Source: Great British Chefs

Lemon Cheesecake

Some people think destiny is something you cannot escape, such as death or a curdled cheesecake, both of which always turn up sooner or later - Lemony Snicket (The Penultimate Peril)

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Digestive Biscuits (crushed) 15
Light soft brown sugar 1/2 tbsp
Butter (melted) 50g
Mascarpone cheese 250g
Single cream 200ml
Condensed milk 397g
Lemons (zested, juiced) 3

Steps

  1. Stir together the digestive biscuits, soft brown sugar and melted butter
  2. Empty into a 23cm springform cake tin and press out level
  3. Leave to chill in the fridge while you make the base (use a smaller tin if you want a thicker base)
  4. Mix together, really well, the mascarpone cheese, cream, condensed milk and lemon zest
  5. Slowly add the lemon juice to the mix a wee bit at a time
  6. Use an electric mixer and spend about 10 minutes mixing these ingredients together (the longer you mix, the thicker the topping gets in the bowl)
  7. Pour onto your chilled base, then return to the fridge for a few hours for it to set

Tips

  • Making it the evening before you need it and then it should be set perfectly
  • Adding a few gingernut biscuits to the base adds a different taste

Sticky Toffee Pudding

I think when people have pudding and jobs, they vote for you - Emmanuel Macron

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Fresh dates (stoned) 225g
Bicarbonate of soda 1 tbsp
Unsalted butter (softened) 200g
Large eggs 2
Self-raising flour 170g
Ground mixed spice 1/4 tbsp
Ground cinnamon 1/4 tbsp
Ovaltine 2 tbsp
Natural yoghurt 2 tbsp
Light muscovado sugar 115g
Double cream 140ml
Ovaltine 2 tbsp

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 180ºC/350ºF/gas 4
  2. Put the dates in a bowl with the bicarbonate of soda and cover with 200ml/7fl oz of boiling water
  3. Leave to stand for a couple of minutes to soften, then drain
  4. Whiz the dates in a food processor until you have a purée
  5. Cream your butter and sugar until pale using a wooden spoon, and add the eggs, flour, mixed spice, cinnamon and Ovaltine
  6. Mix together well, then fold in the yoghurt and your puréed dates
  7. Pour into a buttered, ovenproof dish and bake in the preheated oven for 35 minutes
  8. While the pudding is cooking, make the toffee sauce by putting the butter, sugar and cream in a pan over a low heat until the sugar has dissolved and the sauce has thickened and darkened in colour
  9. To serve, spoon out the pudding at the table and pour over the toffee sauce

Tips

  • It should be served warm, not piping hot, about 30 minutes after it has been made
  • Leftovers can be used as snacks like gingerbread, cold or warm

Source: Jamie Oliver

Caffè Mocha

Coffee is the common man's gold, and like gold, it brings to every man the feeling of luxury and nobility. Where coffee is served, there is race and splendor and friendship and happiness. All cares vanish as the coffee cup is raised to the lips - Sheikh Abd-Al-Kadir (In praise of coffee)

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Shot hot espresso (or strong coffee) 1
Chocolate syrup 3 tbsp
Steam milk 1 cup
Vanilla extract 1/4 tbsp
Whipped cream 1 can
Cinnamon 2 tbsp

Steps

  1. In a large coffee mug, pour in 2 tablespoons of chocolate syrup
  2. Add coffee or espresso with vanilla extract and stir together
  3. Add steamed milk
  4. You can stir gently or leave layered
  5. Topped off with whipped cream and 1 tablespoon of syrup
  6. Add cinnamon

Tips

  • Add the cocoa powder to the milk before foaming, for a more chocolate-laden experience
  • It's important to weigh out your espresso grounds. If you don't have enough grounds, your espresso will filter through too quickly and taste weak
  • You can either insert a thermometer into the milk while you're steaming or hold you hand on the side of the pitcher as you steam. Once the pitcher becomes too hot to touch, move your hand away and continue to steam the milk for a few more seconds

Rhubarb & Custard Cocktail

A cocktail done right can really show your guests that you care - Danny Meyer

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Caster sugar 85g
Rhubarb (chopped) 300g
Vodka 100ml
Rhubarb syrup 100ml
Rhubarb (length) 1
Advocaat 30ml
Lemonade 75ml

Steps

  1. To make the rhubarb syrup, put the caster sugar in a saucepan with 75ml water
  2. Heat very gently, so the sugar disappears but the water doesn’t bubble
  3. Tip in the chopped rhubarb, cover with a tight-fitting lid and increase the heat a little so it begins to boil
  4. Simmer, covered, for about 5 mins until the rhubarb is very tender but not disintegrating
  5. Turn off the heat, and tip the pan’s contents into a fine sieve over a bowl
  6. Use a wooden spoon to gently squeeze the rhubarb so that you get all the juices, but no pulp
  7. Tip back into the pan and boil rapidly for a few mins until just syrupy
  8. Pour into a jug (you should have about 125ml) and leave to cool completely
  9. Chill 4 cocktail glasses with ice or in your freezer
  10. Shake the vodka and 100ml of the rhubarb syrup together with ice until mixed and chilled down
  11. Make small shreds of rhubarb using a vegetable peeler and place into iced water (this will make them curl up)
  12. In a separate jug, whisk together the advocaat and lemonade with more ice to lighten
  13. Divide the vodka and rhubarb mixture between the glasses, straining through a small sieve to catch the ice
  14. Carefully pour 25ml of your advocaat mixture into each glass over an upsidedown spoon (the mixture should float on the surface of the vodka syrup mixture)
  15. Drain the rhubarb shreds well and use to garnish your cocktails

Tips

  • When squeezing citrus, it is important to squeeze them just before using them. Acidic fruits oxidise quickly and can turn bitter
  • We eat with our eyes (well, in this case, we drink with our eyes) so garnishing is crucial. This is where you can really make or break a drink. Gone are the days of cherries soaked in syrup, rather garnish your drink with ingredients that have been used to make it
  • To make these swanky citrus twists all you need is a super sharp paring knife and some nimble fingers, start peeling the rind in a circular motion around your fruit until you have a long strip, rub the rind on the rim of your glass to add some zesty flavour, now twist the citrus rind around your finger, or a skewer for tighter spirals and remove carefully

Watermelon Lemonade Slush

Life is like eating a watermelon, you know you're going to get some seeds; just spit them out and take another bite - Jeff Steinmann

Ingredients

Ingredients Amounts
Cubed seedless watermelon (chilled) 6 cups
Cold water 4 cups
Lemon juice (strained, chilled) 3/4 cup
Granulated sugar 2/3 cup
Ice As much as you like
Fresh mint As much as you like

Steps

  1. Add watermelon to a blender and pulse until well pureed
  2. Pour through a fine mesh strainer into a bowl
  3. In a large pitcher whisk together water, lemon juice and sugar until sugar has dissolved
  4. Stir in pureed watermelon
  5. Stir in ice and mint and store in refrigerator until ready to serve

Tips

  • Instead of stirring ice and mint you can alternately add ice and mint directly to individual cups and pour lemonade over
  • If you don't have time to chill the ingredients then just use more ice in place of some of the water

Source: Cooking Classy
